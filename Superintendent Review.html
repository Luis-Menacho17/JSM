What the team celebrated
Strengths and bright spots acknowledged during the September review
Student goal-setting: Kudos given on student goal conferences — the idea that students should know their reading/math levels and have a personal roadmap to success was explicitly praised.
Academic growth: A 9% jump in math and ELA and a 28% jump in science were highlighted as strong progress.
DataWise & professional learning: The approach to using DataWise school-wide and intentionality around coaching cycles and learning walks was recognized.
Community engagement structures: Student Council's role in surveying students and bringing results to the SPMT was highlighted. Alumni and career day programming appreciated.
Veteran teacher culture: The strong group of veteran teachers acting as mentors for newer staff was noted as a school asset.
Questions raised & areas the team pushed on
What the supportive colleagues and critical friends raised during the session
Tracking community events: "Do you take surveys from school events (fun days) to see if they help? How do you track?" — The answer was that Student Council surveys students and brings results to the SPMT.
Adult actions behind academic gains: "Has the team unpacked the adult actions that helped with the increase?" — A direct ask to connect adult practice to student outcomes.
Family engagement & immigration climate: "What does family engagement look like, specifically with the current social climate around immigration?" — Team was asked to think collectively about how to reach families with current fears, and how to leverage Apptegy for that.
Reluctant families: "What plan do you have to get reluctant parents/families into the school?" — Response: Frontload with academic activities (literacy/math coach in-service at home) before fun family events, rather than fun first.
Barriers to attendance: "What barriers remain for children who don't want to come to school?" — Identified as school culture & climate. Noted that CA was ~10% pre-pandemic and rose to 27% after.
Accountability vs empathy in attendance: "How do you balance accountability vs empathy?" — Response: Wrap-around services, making school inviting, reopening the pool as an incentive, individual goal-setting with students.

How JSM responded to the first review's direction over the following six months
Student data cards distributed after Fall and Winter benchmarks — every student can articulate their RIT score and growth goal.
Bilingual math newsletters sent home after every unit — English and Spanish — directly connecting to the "parents should know the plan" directive.
DataWise expanded school-wide (was Grade 8 pilot only in 24–25). Literacy named as schoolwide focus; grade-band problems of practice active in all grades.
iReady Winter: 72% of students making typical annual math growth — 3rd in the district. Math growth from Sep to Jan: students 3+ grades behind dropped from 34% to 20%.
Spanish literacy (Lectura K–3): well-below readers dropped from 52% to 35% school-wide. Kindergarten alone went from 65% to 25% well-below — directly in response to ML support needs.
Community partners grew from 9 (Fall 2025) to 14+ (March 2026). Breakfast with Bigs, VT Leadership Trip (37 students), Career Day, alumni program all active.
Family engagement gap remains — Apptegy transition caused friction, family climate survey at 9% response. Actively working multi-channel outreach strategy for spring.
